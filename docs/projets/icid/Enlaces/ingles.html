10 must - see places in Uruguay

Punta del Diablo

-Location:

The capital of Uruguay, Montevideo, is located 289 km from Punta del Diablo. To get there you must first take the Interbalnearia Route, and then continue along Route 9. You also have the option of traveling by bus from the Tres Cruces Terminal or from other Uruguayan beaches. During the summer the frequency of the buses is greater.

History:

According to the records, Punta del Diablo was born in the mid-30s and 40s of the 20th century, when the first groups of settlers began to build houses in the area. At that time, that population was mostly fishermen and residents of Valizas and other nearby spas.

Activities to do:

In Punta del Diablo you can enjoy the sea at Playa de los Pescadores, La Viuda and Playa del Rivero. After that, take a moment to tour its craft fair, located in the center of town. There, artisans and artists add color and life to the fair, while tourists visit the stalls and buy souvenirs from the trip. At the end of the day, give yourself the luxury of witnessing a wonderful sunset and, for dinner, be sure to enjoy a good meal based on seafood.

Cost of life:

The prices, although they rise in high season, are accessible. The lifestyle in Punta del Diablo is relaxed, without too many luxuries, so you can spend an unforgettable stay without spending too much.

Colonia

-Location:

On the northern shore of the Río de la Plata estuary, upstream from Montevideo, Colonia del Sacramento is located at the end of a peninsula washed on three sides. Its central part is a little higher and its relief descends towards the coasts.

History:

Colonia del Sacramento was founded by Don Manuel Lobo, Governor of Rio de Janeiro, appointed by Prince Pedro II of Portugal, to create a point of defense on the east coast of the Río de la Plata.

Activities to do:

  • Go fishing
  • Take pictures
  • Walk along the boulevard

Santa Teresa Fortress

-Location:

The Santa Teresa Fortress is located at 305 kilometers from Montevideo and 36 kilometers from the international locality of chuy on the frontier between Brazil and Uruguay

History:

The Fortress is now part of the national park of Santa Teresa, created to protect it

The current Santa Teresa Fortress was built by the Spanish crown, although its foundation and name are due to Portugal. In the short period of October from 1765 to 1775 three fortifications were built.

The signing of the Treaty of El Pardo (1761) effectively annulled the Treaty of Madrid (1750), so the governor and captain general of the captaincy of Rio de Janeiro, Gomes Freire de Andrade, count of Bobadela, anticipated the consequences of it for the southern region, which he knew well. He ordered the governor of the Colonia do Río Grande de São Pedro, Colonel Elói Madureira, to immediately send troops from Laguna to the Linea de Castillos Grande. The troops were commanded by Lieutenant Colonel Tomás Luis de Osorio, commander of the Cavalry troops of the Dos Dragones Regiment and of Fort Jesús, María, José de Río Pardo.

Gathering just over a thousand men, the Portuguese strategy was to quickly build a fortified defensive line, south of Fort San Miguel on Chuí Stream, to stop the Spanish invasion in progress, after the conquest of Colonia del Sacramento in October 1762 by the governor of Buenos Aires, Pedro de Ceballos, at the head of nearly three thousand men gathered in Maldonado.

Osorio had a field fortification built in December 1762, garnishing it with about 400 soldiers and artillerying it with some small caliber pieces. The place chosen was the Angostura gorge near the Castillos Grande mountain, closing the land road along the coast, which went from Colonia del Sacramento to the town of Río Grande.

Activities to do:

There are a lot of activites to do in the fortress and outside of it, here i'll tell you a few:

  • Guided Tours: You can take guided tours of the fortress to learn about its history, architecture and its role in the past.
  • Explore the Site: You can tour the fortress on your own, exploring its walls, cannons, and defensive structures. This will allow you to immerse yourself in history and enjoy panoramic views of the city and the coast.
  • Museum: At times, the fortress has housed a small museum displaying historical artifacts and exhibits related to the history of Uruguay and the fortress itself.
  • Cultural and Recreational Events: The Santa Teresa Fortress has hosted several cultural events, such as open-air concerts, theater performances, and art exhibitions. These events may vary depending on the season and planned activities.
  • Hiking and Nature: In addition to its historical value, the fortress is located in a beautiful natural environment. You can enjoy walks on the nearby trails and appreciate the native flora and fauna.
  • Picnics and Rest: Many people enjoy taking food and drinks for a picnic in the open spaces of the fortress. It is a quiet place to relax and enjoy the surroundings.
  • Photography: The architecture and panoramic views offered by the Santa Teresa Fortress are ideal for photography lovers.
  • Bird Watching: If you are a fan of bird watching, the fortress and its surroundings could offer you opportunities to spot different species.

Cost of life:

The cost to enter the Fortress is 50 Uruguayan Pesos and people that are less than 12 and more than 65 years old can enter free

Piriapolis

-Location:

Piriápolis is 97 kilometers from Montevideo. To get there from the capital, the easiest thing to do is to come along the Interbalnearia Route and take the detour located at Km. 82.
Although we recommend accessing before, through the Solís Spa (Km. 80) and get to Piriápolis by Route 10, ensuring a beautiful panoramic tour of the seaside resorts that precede it along the coast.

History:

Piriápolis was founded in 1893 by Francisco Piria, conceived from the beginning as a European-style tourist destination.

Activities to do:

It has all the facilities of a big city, but without losing the calm of the spas, with large hills, beautiful beaches and many places full of fun. Whether to visit with family, with your group of friends or alone.

Cost of life:

Depending on the season. There are small hotels starting at $50 per room, although in high season the average is $100 per room. To spend many days, renting a house is a good option, and you can find excellent residences for USD $1,500 a fortnight.

La Paloma

-Location:

The city is located in the southern part of the department of Rocha, in Cape Santa María, and at the crossroads of national routes 10 and 15. It is 240 km from Montevideo, and 90 km from Punta del Este.

History:

La Paloma's foundation date is December 12, 1884. According to historians where the police station works today, it would have previously been the site of a Manuel Oribe ranch.
In 1884 Lieutenant Colonel Parallada, then owner of the place known as La Paloma, began to sell small fractions of his land, boosting the population of these lands. The town was not delineated by surveyors, as a consequence, its streets are uneven, and its blocks irregularly shaped.

Activities to do:

There are kilometers and kilometers of Atlantic coast in all possible physiognomies, from the central bays to the remote beaches to the east and west. You can choose between trendy beaches like La Balconada, familiar places like Bahía Grande and Bahía Chica, natural pools like El Cabito, surfing peaks like La Aguada, Zanja Honda and Corumbá, fishing spots like Los Botes, isolated paradises like Anaconda or wild spas. like Antoniópolis, Costa Azul or Arachania.

Cost of life:

Prices in La Paloma are higher in high season, but remain affordable. There are accommodation offers for all possibilities. Rental houses range from $50 to $200 per day, hotels are around $50 to $80 per day (for two) and campsites are around $10 per person per day. January is the most expensive month to stay: a cabin that costs $500 a fortnight in November costs $2,000 in January.

Food expenses also have several options. There are several restaurants in La Paloma at prices that are not too high, although shopping in supermarkets or delicatessens is the cheapest option. You can spend a comfortable day in La Paloma spending around 20 dollars on food.

Beach Punta del Este

-Location:

The Beach of Punta del Este is locates in the province of Maldonado, in the city of Punta del Este

History:

At the end of the 19th century and the beginning of the 20th century, Punta del Este began to attract the attention of visitors due to its beautiful beaches and pleasant climate. The first summer houses began to be built, and the city became a vacation destination for the upper classes of Uruguay and other countries in the region.

Activities to do:

There you have large amount of activities you can do:

  • Relaxation on the Beach: Enjoy simply relaxing on the sand, sunbathing and listening to the waves of the sea. It is a perfect place to disconnect and rest.
  • Swimming and Bathing: The waters of Punta del Este beach are generally safe for swimming, so you can enjoy a refreshing dip in the sea.
  • Water Sports: The beach offers opportunities to practice a variety of water sports, such as surfing, kitesurfing, windsurfing and paddleboarding. There are schools and rentals available for those who wish to try these activities.
  • Boating: You can rent boats or join boat tours to explore the coast from a different perspective.
  • Scenic Photography: Punta del Este beach offers impressive views, spectacular sunsets and unique photographic opportunities.

Cost of life:

You can stay in 3 star hotels from 1652 UYU to 2256 UYU

Las Termas de Arapey

-Location:

The Termas of Arapey is locates in the province of Salto, 576 kilometers from Montevideo, 80 kilometers from the vity of Salto and 65 kilometers from border crossing that joins Concordia with the Salto Grande dam.

History:

The history of the Termas del Arapey goes back centuries, when the indigenous communities of the region already knew and used the healing properties of the thermal waters. However, its development as a modern tourist destination began in the 20th century.

Activities to do:

There are more activities to do here than you probably think, here you can:

  • Thermal Baths: The main activity in the hot springs is to enjoy baths in the hot springs. These waters are rich in minerals and are believed to have therapeutic properties. You can immerse yourself in pools of different temperatures and relax while the properties of the water act on your body.
  • Spa Treatments: Many thermal complexes in Arapey offer a variety of spa treatments, such as therapeutic massages, body wraps, facials, and more. These treatments can help you relax and revitalize.
  • Outdoor Walks: The Arapey Hot Springs are surrounded by nature. You can take leisurely walks on the surrounding paths, enjoying the greenery and fresh air.
  • Water Sports and Activities: Some complexes offer facilities for water sports and activities such as swimming, aquagym or even yoga classes in the water.
  • Gastronomy: Many complexes have restaurants and places to enjoy healthy food and drinks. You can take the opportunity to try local dishes and healthy options.
  • Recreational Activities: In some cases, the complexes have recreation areas that include sports courts, ping pong tables, indoor games, etc.
  • Rest and Relaxation: The hot springs are an ideal place to simply relax and unwind. You can enjoy quiet readings, meditation or just rest in the rest areas.
  • Excursions and Tours: If you wish to explore beyond the hot springs, excursions to nearby places of interest, such as natural, cultural or historical attractions, may be offered
  • Classes and Workshops: At some resorts, classes and workshops may be offered, such as healthy cooking classes, yoga, meditation, or other activities focused on wellness.
  • Special Events: Depending on the season and the resort, special events may be organized, such as outdoor concerts, theme nights and entertainment activities.

Cost of life:

The entrance can cost from 3395 Uruguayan pesos to 9973

La Pedrera

-Location:

La Pedrera is a Uruguayan seaside resort on the coast of the department of Rocha. It is located on the Atlantic Ocean, 230 km east of Montevideo.

History:

La Pedrera is one of the oldest resorts on the Uruguayan coast, beginning with the rock formations on its beach, which are more than 600 million years old. The erosion of the sea and the wind has given capricious and striking shapes to the stones of the spa, which give it its name as well as being its main postcard.

Activities to do:

  • Horseriding
  • Swim
  • Sightseeing
